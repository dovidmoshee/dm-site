# Cohevo Explainer

Cohevo helps small SaaS startups and agencies install the operating layer their business runs on: mapped workflows, the right tool stack, tested automations, useful AI workflows, and clear documentation.

The simple version: most growing teams do not need another random AI tool. They need their work to stop leaking between people, tabs, spreadsheets, Slack threads, CRMs, project tools, and founder memory. Cohevo fixes that in a focused 60-day engagement.

## One-line explanation

Cohevo installs the operating system your business runs on, in 60 days.

## 30-second explanation

Cohevo works with SaaS startups and agencies with 3 to 20 people whose operations have started to lag behind their growth. The team may have customers, revenue, and ambition, but the internal system is messy: processes live in people's heads, tools do not talk to each other, data gets copied around manually, and the founder is still pulled into every handoff.

Cohevo maps how the business actually works, designs the right stack, builds the automations that connect it, adds AI only where it saves real time, and hands the whole thing back with SOPs and training.

## Who Cohevo is for

Cohevo is built for teams that are already working, already selling, and already feeling the cost of weak internal systems.

Best-fit clients:

- SaaS startups with 3 to 20 people
- Agencies with 3 to 20 people
- Teams with existing revenue and manual operations
- Founders who are still the bottleneck for basic handoffs
- Teams using five or more tools that do not connect cleanly
- Businesses where onboarding, sales follow-up, reporting, or delivery updates are getting slower as the team grows

Cohevo is probably too early for pre-revenue teams, solo experiments, and companies that do not yet know what they sell or how they deliver it.

## The problem Cohevo solves

Small teams often grow faster than their operating system.

At first, that is normal. The founder knows everything. The team can ask questions in Slack. Customers are handled manually. A spreadsheet is good enough. A Zapier automation here and a Notion page there feel like progress.

Then the cracks show.

- Leads arrive, but follow-up depends on memory.
- Customer onboarding changes depending on who handles it.
- Reporting takes hours because data is spread across tools.
- New hires need constant explanation because nothing is written down.
- The team has many subscriptions, but no clear source of truth.
- AI tools are added randomly instead of being built into real workflows.

The business is not broken. The operating layer is missing.

## What Cohevo builds

Cohevo's core offer is the Business OS Setup, a 60-day structured engagement.

It produces five main deliverables:

1. Business Systems Map
   A visual audit of every workflow, tool, handoff, and failure point in the business.

2. Tool Stack Architecture
   A clear recommendation for which tools to keep, connect, replace, or ignore. Every choice is tied to the team's actual workflow, not software trends.

3. Automation Layer
   Live automations that move work between tools. Examples: lead forms into CRM, CRM updates into onboarding tasks, notifications for handoffs, reporting updates, and follow-up reminders.

4. AI Workflow Layer
   AI added only where it saves time in the day-to-day business. Examples: meeting summaries, support draft generation, research assistance, reporting summaries, and internal knowledge retrieval.

5. Documentation and Training
   SOPs, walkthroughs, and a live training session so the team can run the system without Cohevo after handoff.

## How the 60-day process works

Cohevo runs through four phases.

### 1. Discover

The work starts with the real business, not the tool list. Cohevo audits the current tools, interviews the team about workflows, and identifies where time is being lost.

Output: Business Systems Map.

### 2. Design

Using the map, Cohevo designs the target operating layer: tools, handoffs, automation logic, AI use cases, ownership, and documentation structure. The client reviews and approves the design before build work starts.

Output: Tool Stack Architecture and build plan.

### 3. Implement

Cohevo builds the approved system in the client's live environment. Automations are connected, AI workflows are configured, and each flow is tested end to end.

Output: working automations, AI workflows, configured tools, and tested handoffs.

### 4. Train

The engagement ends only when the client's team can operate the system. Cohevo writes SOPs, records or walks through the system, trains the team, and refines based on feedback.

Output: documented system, team training, clean handoff.

## What changes for the client

Before Cohevo, the client usually has motion but not leverage. Work is happening, but too much depends on people remembering what to do.

After Cohevo, the client should have:

- Clear workflows the team can see and follow
- Fewer manual copy-paste steps
- Better handoffs between sales, onboarding, delivery, and support
- One clearer source of truth for pipeline, tasks, and customer status
- AI built into real work instead of sitting as a separate toy
- SOPs that make delegation and onboarding easier
- A founder who is pulled into fewer routine decisions

Realistic outcomes vary, but the target pattern is 8 to 15 hours saved per week across the team, onboarding time reduced by 40 to 60 percent, and fewer wasted tools.

## What Cohevo is not

Cohevo is not a custom software agency.

It does not usually write heavy custom code, rebuild a product, manage marketing campaigns, or become a permanent operations team. The normal build uses no-code and low-code tools such as Zapier, Make, n8n, Notion, HubSpot, Airtable, Slack, Linear, Intercom, and similar SaaS platforms.

Cohevo can flag when custom engineering is needed, but the main offer is about installing the practical operating layer around the business, not building bespoke software from scratch.

## Why the offer works

The offer works because it starts with workflows, not tools.

Most messy teams try to fix operations by buying another app. That usually creates more surfaces to manage. Cohevo starts by mapping how work moves through the business. Only after that does it decide what should be automated, what should be documented, what should be owned by a person, and what should be ignored.

The result is a system the team can actually use. Not a giant transformation project. Not a pile of generic SOPs. Not AI for its own sake. A small-business operating layer that is good enough to run on Monday morning.
