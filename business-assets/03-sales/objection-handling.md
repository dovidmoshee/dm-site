# Objection Handling — Cohevo

## "This is too expensive"
Response: Understood. We can reduce scope to the Foundation package and phase the rest. The Foundation covers the audit, architecture, and up to 5 core automations — the highest-leverage starting point for most teams.

## "We need to think about it"
Response: Makes sense. What specific concern should we resolve first — scope, timeline, or investment?

## "We can do this in-house"
Response: You probably can. The value here is speed, outside perspective, and clean implementation without pulling your team off their actual work. Most clients try to do it internally and end up back here 6 months later.

## "Now is not the right time"
Response: Fair. The teams that wait usually do so because they're too busy — which is exactly the symptom the engagement fixes. That said, I'd rather you start when it's genuinely right. What milestone or date would make this a priority?

## "We already have a CRM / tools set up"
Response: That is the most common starting point. The audit phase begins with what you have and builds from what is already working. Nothing gets replaced without a clear reason and a better option ready to go.

## "Do we need 60 days? Can you do this faster?"
Response: The 60-day structure exists so the build is tested, your team is trained, and nothing is rushed. Most of the time is implementation and refinement, not discovery. We can discuss compressing the timeline if your situation calls for it, but I won't compromise on testing and documentation.
